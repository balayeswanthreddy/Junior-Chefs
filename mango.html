Ingredients:

  • Raw mangoes (firm and sour) - 1 kg
  • Salt - 4-5 tbsp (adjust to taste)
  • Red chili powder - 3-4 tbsp
  • Turmeric powder - 1 tsp
  • Mustard seeds - 2 tbsp
  • Fenugreek seeds (methi) - 1 tsp
  • Asafoetida (hing) - a pinch
  • Mustard oil - 1 cup
     ->Preparation:
       1.Wash and dry the mangoes thoroughly.Cut the mangoes into
         small pieces when it has no moisture on the mangoes.
       2.You can keep the skin on or remove it based on preference.
     ->Sun-drying the mangoes:
       1.Spread the mango pieces on a clean,dry cloth or a large plate.
       2.Let them dry in the sun for a few hours until the moisture 
         evaporates
     ->Preparing the spice mix:
       1.Heat a small pan and dry roast mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds 
         separately until they turn aromatic. Allow them to cool down.
       2.Grind the roasted mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds into a  powder
         using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. 
       3.In a large mixing bowl,combine the salt,red chilipowder, 
         turmericpowder,asafoetida, and the ground mustard-fenugreek powder.  
     ->Preparing the pickle:
       1.Take the dried mango pieces and coat them evenly with the spice 
         mix prepared in the previous step.
       2.Place the spice-coated mango pieces in a clean, dry glass jar. 
       3.Heat mustard oil in a small pan until it reaches its smoking point.
         Turn off the heat and allow it to cool down to room temperature.
       4.Once the oil cools down, pour it over the mango pieces in the jar.
       5.Seal the jar tightly with a lid.
       6.Place the jar in a warm and dry place for about a week
       7.Shake the jar gently every day to ensure even distribution of
         spices and oil.
       8.Once the pickle matures,store it in a cool.The mango pickle can be 
         consumed after a week but tastes even better as it pass over time.
