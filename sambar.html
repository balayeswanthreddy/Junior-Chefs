Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup toor da
  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves and 4 curry leaves for garnish
       1.Wash the toor dal thoroughly and pressure cook it with 
       2 cups of water until it's soft and mushy. Set it aside.
       2.Soak the tamarind in 1 cup of warm water for about 10-15 minutes. 
         Squeeze the tamarind to extract the pulp.Discard the pulp and  
         seeds, and keep the tamarind water aside.
       3.Heat oil or ghee in a large pot or kadai. Add mustard seeds and  
         let them splutter.Then add cumin seeds,asafoetida,and curry leaves
         Sauté for a few seconds.
       4.Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions 
         turn translucent.
       5.Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
       6.Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a couple of minutes.
       7.Add sambar powder and turmeric powder. Mix well and 
         sauté for a minute.
       8.Pour in the tamarind water along with 2-3 cups of water.
       9.Add salt to taste and bring the mixture to a boil.let it simmer 
         until the vegetables are cooked.
       10.Once the vegetables are cooked, add the cooked dal to the pot 
          and mix well. 
       11.Adjust the consistency of the sambar by adding water if needed.
       12.Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.  
                Your simple homemade sambar is ready to be served
